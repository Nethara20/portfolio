Around 300 CE, engineers near here invented the bisokotuwa — a valve pit that controlled the outflow pressure of massive reservoirs using nothing but stone and geometry. Europe wouldn't solve the same problem until the 1700s. A few generations later, King Dhatusena's canal from Kalawewa carried water 87 kilometers to Anuradhapura on a gradient of roughly six inches per mile — precise enough that parts of it still function today. I grew up in that lineage. I build interfaces the same way they built water systems: pressure, flow, and precision, engineered so it just works.
